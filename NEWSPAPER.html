Oldie City Avenue

The City Chronicles

Mysterious Figure in Hat Spotted on Midnight Dock

Several night watchmen report seeing a tall, overcoated figure standing alone on Pier 9 just after midnight. Witnesses say the brim of his hat hid his face, and that he vanished into the harbor fog when approached. Authorities suspect a link to recent cargo thefts, though no arrest has been made. Locals whisper the name Night Tide.

New Seagull camera is a popular hit!!

The newly released Seagull Model 4 has taken the city by storm, with shop windows cleared within hours of arrival. Produced by Barrow & Co., the compact wonder boasts a faster shutter, improved glass lens, and a frame so light it can be carried in a coat pocket. Photographers praise its ability to “catch a moment before it flies,” while amateur shutterbugs delight in the simplicity of its winding mechanism. Street vendors report queues forming at dawn, with customers eager to secure one before the next shipment arrives. Dealers caution stocks may not last through the month. Society papers are already filling with crisp portraits and candid scenes snapped on the Seagull, cementing its place as this season’s must-have marvel.

Cars and the Jaywalking law

Since the City Council’s passage of the new jaywalking ordinance last month, motorists and pedestrians alike have been adjusting to the letter of the law. The measure, intended to curb the rising number of street accidents, requires citizens to cross only at marked intersections, under penalty of fine. Police report issuing over two hundred citations in the first week alone, though some residents grumble the rule favors automobile owners over foot traffic. “We’ve walked this way for years without trouble,” one shopkeeper complained. Motorists, however, applaud the change, citing fewer sudden stops and near-collisions.Officials urge cooperation, warning that enforcement will continue in earnest as the city’s streets grow busier with the steady hum of engines.
